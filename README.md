## About Me

I'm a climate science graduate with an MSc in Applied Meteorology and Climate from the University of Reading. My work focuses on analysing large-scale atmospheric and oceanic systems using climate model output to explore how regional climate patterns evolve under different Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs). My Master's dissertation explored the impacts of an Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) collapse on hydroclimate systems in Southern Africa using Python-based climate model analysis. The analysis focused on identifying spatial changes in precipitation and temperature variability across different climate scenarios.

My analysis typically involves Python-based workflows for handling large climate datasets, statistical analysis, and visualization, alongside geospatial analysis and mapping using a combination of ArcGIS Pro and Python. 

Alongside technical work, I'm passionate about science communication and translating complex environmental data into clear insights for audiences of varying backgrounds. My BA in environmental studies from the University of Pittsburgh also strengthened my skills in environmental communication.
